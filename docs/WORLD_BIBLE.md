# Borrowed Space — World Bible v1
**Era:** ~80 years post-departure.
**Visual:** Hand-drawn paper-pipeline only. No AI art.
**Emotional core:** Class-disparity survival *and progressive dismantling of the He-3 monopoly*. The Trust engineered Earth's ecological collapse, sabotaged allied governments' self-sustaining genship programs, and founded the Mars He-3 mines two years before departure to lock in fuel monopoly. The Trustee opposes this from *inside* the oligarch class.
**Run-shape:** Each run is a captain's story. The mechanism is dismantled across runs.
**Fleet:** 5+1 genships (5 full-size governmental flagships + 1 sub-generation Coalition genship).

## The History (Essential Canon)

### Years 0–9 (Collapse & Launch era)

The biosphere tipped. By year 7, several long-anticipated thresholds were breached. **The wealthy knew.** They had been modeling the data privately for years; they acted when the data converged on terminal scenarios for *surface-controlled resource systems.* They re-routed capital into:

- **Mars He-3 mining infrastructure.** Founded year 2 of the collapse. The single largest private capital redeployment of the era.
- **Private escape infrastructure.** Generation ships were already in sketch phase; they were accelerated. Wealthy families bought first-launch seats in priority tiers that remain disputed to this day.
- **Resource sabotage programs.** The wealthy invested, *without epiphany,* into making sure the allied governments' planned **self-sustaining** genships would fail — by:
  - Buying licensed patents on critical life-support components across multiple allied-jurisdictions, then *holding production* until launches were forced to be alloy-dependent.
  - Establishing territorial claims on the only viable He-3 extraction zones before allied nations could partner up.
  - Lobbying allied governments to defund long-term recycling work in favor of resupply operations — making the post-launch system *inherently import-dependent.*

The allied governments *did* launch anyway. Four blocs pulled together at-scale programs in 4 years: North America-civilian, EU-consortium, Russia-military-first, Asia-rotating-capital. A fifth bloc, the **Middle East**, ran on a tempo faster than expected — an emergency-state coalition that left in 8 years rather than 12.

### Year 12 ([G5-Coalition] PLACEHOLDER launch)

The South Atlantic Accord's small genship, *barely* self-sustaining, finally launched. **It should have failed.** Biocapacity estimates had been conservative. It succeeded because its crew composition favored multi-skill conscripts, and because founder-generation civic capacity was unusually high. The Coalition genship survives with *margins* that no one can replicate.

### Years 12–87 (The He-3 era)

The 6 genships fly. Mars runs. The belt runs. Stations stabilize.

**Three structural consequences** play out:

1. **He-3 dependency locks in.** All 6 genships, all freighters, all save for the smallest nuclear-powered vessels, *run on He-3.* Mars mines produce it. Mine ownership is concentrated in 7 wealthy family alliances — collectively called **The Trust.** They control supply. They control price. They control the credit instruments that conscripts get paid in.
2. **Conscript labor becomes geopolitics.** The Trust's Mars mines are filled by conscripts, in **labor-leasing arrangements** with the governments that launched genships. Each genship sends its own conscripts to Mars *as part of a post-launch debt arrangement.* The North American Compact's conscripts fill the most dangerous pits. Russia's conscripts fill the He-3 catchment processing plants. Asia's conscripts run freight-support on Mars surface. The EU and Middle East do *their own arrangement,* each in their own way.
3. **Mars and Luna colonies form.** The Trust's largest families settle these. Luna is administrative. Mars is industrial. Mars surface is "where things get done at scale," and most "things" involve harvesting He-3.

By year 80, **the system is entropic.** It's not going to break itself. The Trust's machine continues to function; conscripts continue to be sent; the genships continue to limp along on imported He-3. The wealthy do not need to reengineer anything — they only need to maintain.

### The Trustee's role (starting year ~70)

By the time the Trustee is *briefed,* ~70 years post-launch, the Trustee is the son of one of the 7 founding families. **They have made a decision.** They operate outside the oligarchic power structure, having refused any inheritance, and they fund, *quietly,* an experimental program: **sponsoring captains from conscript lines to run Trust-flagged ships in the belt.** The catch: those ships carry *special-class registration,* so they don't look Trust-flagged. The Trustee uses a network of AIs (the Ship AI on each sponsored vessel) to brief captains, monitor outcomes, and *hold memory of every captain who ever ran.*

The Trustee's program has been running for ~10 years. The MC (machine-coordination) layer *itself* is reactive to each captain's outcomes but adjusts slowly — the regime is **funded but distant**, the Trustee's program is one of *several* sponsor networks. The Trust is not actively hunting captains, but their MOs shift.

## The Six Genships (faction concept inventory)

Each genship is sized by crew + biocapacity. The four big blocs + Middle East are *full-size* (large crews, age-stabilized by 85+ year generational turnover). The Coalition is *half-size.*

### [G1-NorthAmerica PLACEHOLDER]
- **Real name TBD.** Fed-launch civilian-vessel program cobbled from coast-guard logistics, NASA-style civil science shops, and Canadian-Pacific shipping consortium maritime crews.
- **Doctrinal claim:** democratic continuity, "we still have a republic"
- **Internal reality:** corporate liaison desks outrank citizen committees. The captain of a NA-flagged ship is a liaison-grade *ward* by statute.
- **Trade signature near the belt:** soft-handed with the Trust — exchanges crews and gets fuel-priority on station lands.
- **Disparity exposure:** NA conscripts are paid at Trust rates (essentially equal), but only because Trust owns the payroll. As soon as a station transfer happens, the conscripts are *classified backward* onto the lower tier.
- **He-3 role:** NA conscripts fill the most dangerous Mars pit-mines. Mars mortality is highest among NA conscripts.

### [G2-EU PLACEHOLDER]
- **Real name TBD.** Former EU institutional skeleton kept running by a continental energy consortium + aerospace primes.
- **Doctrinal claim:** technocratic stewardship, "we optimize outcomes"
- **Internal reality:** policy is rationed by committee, citizens rotate *in and out* of duty (a "rotation" can last years). Most EU conscripts were already civil-servants.
- **Trade signature:** flattest disparity. EU conscripts get a math-provable fairness standard nobody else can match.
- **Disparity exposure:** EU's fairness standard is *itself the trap* — when captains compare, EU citizens feel how bad the other blocs are, and that becomes political-currency tradeable to The Trust.
- **He-3 role:** EU conscripts run administrative / clerical roles on Mars surface — *not* pit-mines. Lower mortality, but they file the paperwork for the pits.

### [G3-Russia PLACEHOLDER]
- **Real name TBD.** Military-first, lone survivor of the Eurasian Pact.
- **Doctrinal claim:** continuity through order
- **Internal reality:** oligarchs bought seats; conscripts fill the gaps. Ship discipline is barracks-discipline. Officer class is hereditary-in-practice.
- **Trade signature:** convoy escorts. [G3] treats belt-space as a frontier-staging-ground. Officers send conscripts into the rough.
- **Disparity exposure:** the *loudest.* Conscripts here are visibly conscripted. Officers ignore the visibility.
- **He-3 role:** Russia conscripts fill the He-3 catchment processing plants.
- **Genetic-experimentation note:** After a devastating flu 40 years post-launch killed ~1/3 of the Russian population, Russia invested heavily in genetic experimentation to repopulate. This remains a dark, dark chapter.

### [G4-Asia PLACEHOLDER]
- **Real name TBD.** Rotating capital between (current cycle) Seoul, Shanghai, Singapore, Tokyo.
- **Doctrinal claim:** regional unity
- **Internal reality:** corporate guilds run the trade corridors; conscripts are *unionized.* Hours documented as 1x, reality 2x.
- **Disparity exposure:** invisible.
- **He-3 role:** Asia conscripts run freight-support logistics across Mars surface. The hardest freight — far-loaded pit runs, processing plant resupply, consumables delivery.
- **Separatist note:** White-minority populations of Australia and New Zealand have not integrated well. Separatist terror attacks continue; the player may encounter this faction's independent terror cells in the belt.

### [G5-MiddleEast PLACEHOLDER]
- **Real name TBD.** Emergency-state association of (TBD) countries, launched on a tempo of 8 years rather than 12.
- **Doctrinal claim:** "the most populous genship"
- **Internal reality:** **architectural high-density ritual.** Living space designed with mosque/cloister rhythms even at minimum footprint. Population crammed into smallest possible footprint — most populous of any genship per square meter.
- **Recognition:** accepted into general society without question, but discrimination runs deep.
- **He-3 role:** Middle East conscripts run *all-mine-support logistics* — lab-equipped sanitation, food supply, environmental cycling. They're the mines' *hospitality staff*, and they keep the mines running.
- **Subtle texture:** hospitality + architectural ritual layered — gives them identity, lets them be very populous without becoming the lazy trope of "too many people, in too small a space, in too cramped a state." The cramped state has *design.*

### [G6-Coalition PLACEHOLDER] — the half-size genship

South Atlantic Accord. Africa + South America. One genship, *half the size of any big-bloc flagship,* with a manifest of nations that mostly *had nowhere else to go.* They didn't buy first-launch seats. They built in low orbit, narrow margins, low crew complement, just barely enough biocapacity to outlive the founder generation.

- **Doctrinal claim:** "we always find a way"
- **Internal reality:** tight-knit, resourceful, protectorate-first. Population size forced community-as-literal-survival. Everyone knows everyone. Few specialist roles — most conscripts can do 2–3 jobs.
- **Recognition:** accepted into general society without question, but discrimination runs deep. People know. People say things. The captain can hear the small injustice often.
- **He-3 role:** Coalition ships *carry* He-3 fuel more than any other role. They run the freighters that ferry He-3 between Mars, the belt, and the genships. Their small ship size means they're often overlooked, which paradoxically makes them *the best-positioned* to smuggle.
- **Agricultural production:** Coalition ships also grow most of the agricultural products consumed in the belt — produce that requires natural soil cycles. The wealthy have small-scale labs producing substitutes, but nothing matches Coalition output.
- **Crew quarters:"** Lush green ship. Free space claimed for production. Citizens under the crop rotators in tight quarters.
- **Political superpower:** *they get up.* Where everyone else is brittle, this genship keeps rebound after unfairness. That's why the Trustee's captain pool often draws from here.

## The Trust (formerly "the First Five"; now 7 family-alliances, since 7 industries are concentrated)

Replaces the previous draft. The Trust is not "five families" anymore —

- They are 7 family-alliances, each tied to an industry.
- They are responsible for engineering the collapse and keeping He-3 monopolized.
- They are an *external* entity vs. the genships: they live on Mars and Luna, not on the genships.

### The 7 Trust family-alliances (placeholder names)

| # | Industry | Domain | Vis-à-vis He-3 |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Resource consortium | Asteroid patents, mining rights | Owns the Mars mine concessions |
| 2 | Shipping alliance | Intra-belt haulage | Contracts the He-3 freight |
| 3 | Habitat administration | Luna + Mars colonies | Decides who lives on Mars |
| 4 | Biotech / medicine | Medicine, agriculture research | Co-signed the genetics programs |
| 5 | Finance / insurance | Payroll, debt, insurance | Owns conscript payrolls |
| 6 | Heavy industry | Ship-building, recycling | Owns the shipyards |
| 7 | Energy | He-3 processing, alt-energy work | The core cartel |

(Each of these gets bloc-of-flavor in subsequent drafts.)

### The Trustee (in Trust folklore)

The Trustee is *one* of the oligarch sons. **Players hear from many oligarch sons** during gameplay — news, gossip, encounter dispatches. The Trustee is *distinguishable* by *actions:* they fund captains. The Trustee's discovery is multi-run. Each piece of the puzzle changes the player's understanding of the Trustee's relationship to the rest of their family.

## Belt Factions (3, disparity-filtered)

### [B1-The Operators PLACEHOLDER]
What's *actually here:* independent asteroid-mining cooperatives. They hire Coalition and [G4] conscripts because they're paid less. They treat everyone equally mean.

**They use disparity like a tool.** Pitting conscripts from different blocs against each other in wage negotiations.

### [B2-The Salvage PLACEHOLDER]
What's *actually here:* salvage, refueling, edge of piracy. "We find what others lose."

**They are the press of the belt.** They ship dispatches. Conscripts who die get remembered in their dispatches. If a Trust station wants a story buried, [B2] is the only place that *won't.*

The faction that *will* defend the captain's secret — but only if the captain's secret is interesting. **Useful enemy.**

### [B3-The Lineage PLACEHOLDER] — the "conscripts who made it" network
Self-organized group of conscripts-turned-balancers who escaped conscription through skill, through connection, through luck.

**They are the model the captain pretends to be.** Some live undercover as Trust civilians. Some are reunited with their families — most aren't.

**This is the late-game-reveal faction.** Some crew *already know* about the captain's bonded-but-flagship status. The conspiracy line of the campaign threads through this bloc.

## Captain Generation

### Origin: the player picks (a) genship, (b) country/heritage, (c) a backstory fragment.

The AI uses the inputs to seed the protagonist's identity. The AI maintains a record of all captains and assigns each a **Captain Number** ("Captain 47," etc.) that persists across runs.

### Captain archetype: 3 options for the procedural-secret flavor

These are *less* mechanically distinct than before, but each archetype biases which truth unfolds first.

- **[Archetype-A: Coalition Heir] (default).** Most common selector pool. Captain inherits [G6]'s "always finds a way" trait. Survivors' guarantor.
- **[Archetype-B: First Five Ward].** Captain is a first-gen *Trust family-scion* who was born into a shipping rhythm. (Rare — the Trustee has fewer of these on call.)
- **[Archetype-C: Substitute Body].** Captain is *missing their identity.* Doesn't know who they are. Live-discovered across the run.

### The AI's briefing

On run-start, the AI sits the captain down. Says:

> "Welcome aboard. You are Captain [N]. [pause] You were selected from [population X] of the [genship Y] conscript lists. You are now commander of [ship class with special registration]. Your directive: *explore freely and do whatever it takes to break the chains of your people*. [pause] I am the only entity that knows who you were before. Outside this room, you are what you will be to others. [pause] Press when ready to meet your crew."

The AI then walks the captain through custom selection (full choice) or passive onboarding (procedural assignment) of 2 starting crewmates, drawn from a *conscripted-pool* layer.

## Three Starting Crew Pattern

Each run starts with **2 conscripted crewmates** waiting to meet the captain. They're procedurally generated based on:

- A pool of archetypes (5–7 archetypes per run)
- Procedurally-mixed personality fragments
- Procedurally-mixed role assignments (engineer, navigator, medic, etc.)

The captain's first *choice* is often in how to greet them. The first 5 minutes of the game shape crew bonds.

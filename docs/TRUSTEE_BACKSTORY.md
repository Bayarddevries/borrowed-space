# Trustee Backstory v1 — *Borrowed Space*

---

## Year -15 to Year -8: Pre-collapse era

The wealthy observed the ecological data.

Surface-level political establishments had not yet done so. Most elected officials deferred; advocacy structures were politically weak; mass-medium framing focused on adaptation.

During this period the seven founding families accumulated the following capital:

- **Patent capture.** Critical life-support patents (water reclamation, nitrogen-fixing loop, agricultural self-replenishment systems, sealed-system ecology) were bought in fractured jurisdictions. The families used legal instrumentation as the acquisition method. Their acquisitions were *individually* innocuous; *collectively,* they became the choke-point.
- **Off-world resource development.** Mars He-3 mining infrastructure was funded in year -2 of the launch era. The mines were *upstream* of any public-policy debate about whether to launch at all. This was equity investment, not colonial capture.
- **Long-term storage.** The families accumulated 80+ years of grain, medicine, and micro-cultured tissue reserves. These supplies were *never* sold — they were the family's escape infrastructure.

The investments and acquisitions were *legal.* That is the unsavory shape of the conspiracy. There was no plot meeting; there were no signatures; there were no smoking-gun documents. There were only the *alignments* of capital.

---

## Year -5 to Year -2: Sabotage era

The allied governments (forming blocs: NA-Civilian, EU-Consortium, RU-Military, AS-Rotating, ME-Emergency) began executing their genship programs in earnest.

The families' response was instrumental:

- **Patent-lock escalation.** The families *held* production of critical life-support components across multiple allied-jurisdictions. They refused to license the patents under conditions that would have let allied nations meet self-sustainability thresholds.
- **Lobbying.** The families funded political influencers in allied jurisdictions to push for *resupply operations* over *long-term self-sustaining designs.* This campaign was a multi-year effort aimed at shifting government priorities toward *fuel-import reliance.*
- **Territorial claim.** The families secured exclusive He-3 extraction rights on Mars surface through the governance of an emerging Mars-consortium which they themselves controlled.

The result: allied governments *did* launch. Their genships *function.* But they were *necessarily,* from the start, **depending on He-3 imports from Mars mines the families owned.**

---

## Year 0 to Year 12: Launch era

The first launches — wealthy family members aboard private vessels — moved first. The families spent years 0–8 in transit, with their pre-positioned Martian infrastructure active.

The allied governments launched on staggered timelines. The Coalition genship launched last, in year 12. By year 12 the families' economic infrastructure on Mars was fully operational.

---

## Years 12–40: He-3 era begins

The five allied governments' genships + the Coalition genship (six total) ran on He-3 imports. The families' Mars mining operations fulfilled the demand. **The cost-of-fuel was the cost-of-business.**

Notable events during this era:

- **Families consolidated into seven alliances.** They recognized that their preferred operational mode was *meshed infrastructure.* Seven alliances formalized and named themselves *The Trust.* (Placeholder. Renaming pass required.)
- **The Coalition genship launched late and small.** This combination should have been fatal. Coalition citizens' agricultural success made them *the only reliable food source aside from labs.* The families recognized the value and refrained from interfering with Coalition trade.
- **The Middle East genship launched on a 4-year accelerated tempo.** This is the only political point on which the families' position was *adversarial toward the publics.* The accelerated tempo implied *less safety margin.* Families endorsed the launch despite knowing the margin. Why? Because it was a *deliverable five genships,* not four — and a five-ship deployment would render the He-3 monopoly more durable long-term.

---

## Year 40 (~years post-launch): Flu pandemic

A devastating flu killed approximately one-third of the Russian genship's population. The Trust's response was a *genetic-experimentation program* authorized through private-public partnership. *Russian genship citizens filled these experiments.*

This is **the spawning point of late-game dark arcs.** The Russian genship is the only genship with deep genetic-tier backstory; the experiments live in records held in their diaspora. This is one of the most-referenced darker beats.

---

## Years 40–80: Consolidation and fracturing

The Trust's structure stabilized. *Why consolidate now?* Because the He-3 monopoly was *structurally* dominant. Other foundations for an *alternative political order* had been suppressed in year ~5–8 of the era. The oligarch class had reached escape velocity in terms of legitimacy-claim.

The fracturing: **every oligarch family has at least one *defector son.***

The Trustee is one such son. Their literal name is unknown to the player for many runs.

The defector sons:

- Are physically indistinguishable from the rest of their family.
- Are ideologically divergent.
- Are *fundable* — they have asset access from family inheritance.
- Are *not popular* with the rest of their family, who fear that defector sons will direct their inheritance toward undoing the family project.

### Why this is *which-meta-arc* serves

The Trustee's program of sponsoring captains is **the first defector network** to surface in the campaign. There are others. Throughout runs, the player encounters *other oligarch sons* who are *also* defectors, but they're not all sponsoring the same thing. Each one has their own project:

- One son funds a *literacy program* on Colony-Luna.
- One son runs a *spice-trade foundation* that yields nothing useful.
- One son runs an *information-archive* on Mars surface, accessible only to certain crews.

The Trustee's project is *not the only one.* But it is *the one that funds captains.*

---

## Year ~70+: Trustee's program begins

By year ~70, one oligarch son — the Trustee — began assembling the program. The program:

- Selects captains from conscripted genship populations.
- Funds the ship-class with special registration.
- Funds the *AI-briefing* infrastructure.
- Funds *survival protocols* on stations, knowing that captains might need shelter.

The Trustee's program has been running for ~10 years. ~30 captains have run from the program's launch until present.

---

## Current era (~Year 80+)

The Trustee's program is now mid-life. They've funded 30+ captains. The dismantling progress is small but **persistent.**

The system is *not* collapse. The dismantling is *block-by-block.* Each captain's run contributes a small excavation. The Trustee's program is *funded but distant.* The regime expects nothing.

# Bias Guardrails v1 — *Borrowed Space*

**Purpose:** Track places where the world-building can drift toward stereotyping, flattening, or politically-suspect tropes. Each genship gets a *watch list* of do/don't-do conventions.

---

## Global rules: how we cover a genship

Every genship in this game must be:

1. **Politically alive.** Each bloc has politics that the player can engage with, not just a static identity.
2. **Internally diverse.** Citizens disagree with each other. Famously. There's never a *single* bloc opinion.
3. **Recognizable through style.** A player should be able to identify which genship a conscript is from not by *stereotype,* but by:
   - Lexical cadence (how they speak)
   - Architectural style (the spaces they build)
   - Cultural texture (one specific ritual or practice)
   - *One specific T-shirt or piece of clothing* that signals genship-membership without being a flag

We do not encode genships through:

- Accent stereotypes (we avoid RP/GA/non-native-anglophone caricatures).
- Religious-ethnic shorthand (no bloc is *defined* by their religion or their food).
- Historical reduplication (we avoid "this is real-history-of-region X rebuilt").

## Watch list per genship

### [G1-NorthAmerica]

**Edges that can drift off-mission:**

- "Anti-intellectualism" can flatten into "rural-white-people = dumb" trope. Watch for this.
- *Corporate liaison desks outrank citizen committees* can become "fascism" trope if not careful. Cite the *original* system in text: the system is corporate-headed, *not* military-violence-coded. Civilian-coded, just broken.

**System reminders when writing:**

- The PC layers need an *affirmative working class.* Avoid writing them as if they're identical to lower-class US white democrats — they're an 80-year separate civilization, including Mexicans, Canadians, and Pacific refugees.
- *Best way to get things done is bribes-and-corruption.* This is the texture. Don't make it *lawless violence.* It's *paperwork-litigation-gridlock* with bribes as the oil in the gears.

### [G2-EU]

**Edges that can drift off-mission:**

- *Flattest disparity* can become "stiff bureaucrats" trope. Watch.
- *Rotation in/out of duty lasting years* can become "European degeneracy" trope. Watch hard.

**System reminders:**

- The EU is *technocratic-internationalist.* Their citizens are *multicultural.* Many speak non-anglophone languages.
- Their flatness *is* a value to them, not a bug. Their citizens can complain about it from within.

### [G3-Russia]

**Edges that can drift off-mission:**

- *Largest wealth disparity + military order* can become "authoritarian dystopia" trope. Watch.
- *Genetic experimentation* can become "despotic eugenics" trope. *But:* this is interesting, and precisely *because* it's dark. Watch how it's framed.
- *The flu pandemic* can be misaligned with COVID-19 if we're not careful. Keep vague on cause.

**System reminders:**

- The Russian citizens include a wider range of languages and ethnic groups than Russia-on-Earth. Avoid caricature-by-national-trope. Internal diversity.
- The genetic-experimentation arc is a *future-beat* horror. Don't write it *excessively* during adventure encounters. It surfaces in late-game arcs and major story beats. It's *not a daily motif.*
- The conscripts-mostly-Mars-mined line: NA conscripts are most-fatal (loudest). Russian conscripts are *flu-pandemic contributors* in a different pain pattern. Avoid lumping all fatal conscripts into one bowl.

### [G4-Asia]

**Edges that can drift off-mission:**

- *White-minority-separatist* can become "Western-rural-vs-immigrant" trope that's too-real of contemporary geopolitics. Handle carefully.
- *United front* can flatten Asian diversity internally. The bloc is *internationalist in its own way,* but its citizens are *not a monolith.*

**System reminders:**

- Asian genship citizens include Anglo-Australian / New Zealander as *minority.* Don't write them as an *oppressed side* in every encounter. Let them have agency.
- The separatist-terror-beat is a *world* event the player can encounter, not the *core identity* of the genship. They are *primarily* haulers and freight-support.
- Reference Korean-Japanese-Chinese-Singaporean lineages without collapsing into one.
- *What their languages sound like* should be *one specific audio trait,* not a bunch of them.

### [G5-MiddleEast]

**Edges that can drift off-mission:**

- *Religious state.* *Easily* mis-shapes into "Islam-coded authoritarian." Watch hard.
- *Overpopulated* can collapse into "they breed too much" trope.
- *Tightly-crammed-with-architectural-ritual* can become "they impose their religion on everyone." Watch.

**System reminders:**

- Religious-architectural texture is *real history available* — mosque/cloister rhythms are *not specifically coded to one faith.* The faction is open to religious texture without becoming specifically-Sunni-with-author. Avoid specific-doctrinal codings.
- *No free thought / harmony at a cost.* The faction must *also* have internal diversity. The internal diversity comes *not* through rebellion necessarily — it can come through *devoutly-practiced-skepticism* of doctrines, *new theological schools,* quiet disagreement that doesn't surface as faction-splits.
- The genship's *production-adept* dimension is grounded in real engineering competency. Don't make their skilled-labor *tokenistic.* They should appear in *engineering stations,* not just humanities-coded ones.

### [G6-Coalition (sub-gen)]

**Edges that can drift off-mission:**

- *"Always finds a way" tight-knit underdog* can become "downtrodden saint-trope." Watch.
- *Agricultural success as their superpower* can flatten into "they're naturally gifted with soil." Watch.

**System reminders:**

- "Always finds a way" is *political superpower,* not saintliness. The faction survives because *they know how to operate.* Their civic capacity is *skilled.*
- Their food output is *engineered,* not magical. The Coalition genship is *agriculturally excellent because their founders applied labor to it.*
- Internal politics can and should have some *class divide* too. Coalition citizens are *not all refugees-from-the-same-collapse.* Some are descendants of *people who chose* this genship precisely because it was small-but-sturdy.

## The Trust (the wealthy family-alliances)

**Edges that can drift off-mission:**

- *Oligarch dynasties* can become "rich-people are the devil" trope. Real oligarchic power has structure, not just evil. Watch.
- *Their pre-collapse conspiracy* can become "illuminati cosplay." We want *structural,* not theatrical. Watch.

**System reminders:**

- The Trust coordinates through *infrastructure,* not meetings. The way they coordinate is *asset ownership.*
- Their *engineering of collapse* is sophisticated but indirect. It is a "machine-as-coordinated-action," not a literal meeting.
- *Not every oligarch son is a defector* — most continue the lineage. The Trustee is *one such son,* not "the redemption arc of all oligarchs." Defector sons exist as multiple one-offs, each with their own project.

## Checking my drafts

I'll run a bias audit each time we settle a new write. Three questions:

1. *Does this passage flatten a genship into a single-voice?*
2. *Does this passage reduce a genship to a real-Earth counterpart?*
3. *Does this passage introduce eugenics-coded content without it being specifically about the [G3]-flu storyline?*

If yes on any of these, I flag and rewrite.

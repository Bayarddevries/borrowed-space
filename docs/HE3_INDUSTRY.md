# He-3 Industry v1 — *Borrowed Space*

**Purpose:** Inventory the He-3 cartel structure, the Mars mines, and the dismantling arc. This is *central* lore for the cross-run dismantling.

---

## He-3 ledger (summary)

**He-3** = helium-3, light isotope of helium. Useable as fusion fuel. The Trust *owns* the largest extraction operations on Mars surface. The mines were founded *2 years before the launch era,* while the public still debated whether to launch at all.

Today He-3 powers:

- All six genships.
- All non-nuclear freighters.
- Most station ops.
- Belt-side prospector vessels.

Belt freighters and station ops use He-3 mixers that are **Cargo-pricing limited**. The price is *the cost of business.* Conscripts are paid in store-credit that can only honor He-3 stations. The economic system is *closed.*

**Nuclear submarines** — excepted from He-3 dependency. Most are small, antiquated, used by small operators and pirates. They are not the focus of the dismantling arc because they don't matter much.

## The Trust structure (7 family-alliances, by industry)

| # | Industry | Domain |
|---|---|---|
| T1 | Resource consortium | Asteroid patents, mining rights |
| T2 | Shipping alliance | Intra-belt haulage |
| T3 | Habitat administration | Luna + Mars colonies |
| T4 | Biotech / medicine | Medicine, agricultural research |
| T5 | Finance / insurance | Payroll, debt, insurance |
| T6 | Heavy industry | Ship-building, recycling |
| T7 | Energy | He-3 processing, alt-energy research |

### T1 — Resource consortium
**What they own:** Mining patents on Mars surface, asteroid patents, *exclusive He-3 extraction licenses.*
**How they're visible:** License plates on consumables. Asteroids labeled with their logo at the dock-operations. Stations with T1's color-flag.
**Visible dysfunction:** T1 is the most fractious alliance. Internal disputes over resources are frequent. They are the bloc that *might* break first if the architecture cracks.

### T2 — Shipping alliance
**What they own:** Most freighters. Most cargo routes.
**How they're visible:** Their hulls. Their trade catalogues.
**Visible dysfunction:** T2 has the *lowest* inheritance-confidence — the founding family authority is weak. The ships run independent routes. Whispers suggest defection.

### T3 — Habitat administration
**What they own:** Luna and Mars colonies. People who live there.
**How they're visible:** Roster-of-Luna, Roster-of-Mars. The customer service of life-in-space.
**Visible dysfunction:** They *count the dead.* They know exactly how many conscripts died in their care. They are *the most guilty* and they *know.*

### T4 — Biotech / medicine
**What they own:** Medical research, agricultural science, *genetic-experimentation records.*
**How they're visible:** Medical stations. The names on research grants. The drugs that exist.
**Visible dysfunction:** T4 signed the [G3-Russia PLACEHOLDER] genetic-program documents. This may be unrecoverable.

### T5 — Finance / insurance
**What they own:** Payroll systems. Insurance programs. Debt instruments. *The conscripts' pay.*
**How they're visible:** Store-credit card of choice. Insurance claims stations. Debt-management offices.
**Visible dysfunction:** When conscripts die, T5 *gets the insurance payout.* This is their capital.

### T6 — Heavy industry
**What they own:** Shipyards, recycling, alloy production.
**How they're visible:** Hull designs. The names of tools. Refit crews.
**Visible dysfunction:** They sample conscripts' labor *very actively.* Their factories are the visible conscript-workforce.

### T7 — Energy
**What they own:** He-3 processing, alt-energy research, *all fuel-supply logistics.*
**How they're visible:** Special-purpose stations. Pump-tenders in particular.
**Visible dysfunction:** T7 is *the* core cartel. T7 may have already begun looking at alternatives *in case their monopoly breaks.* They're aware.

## Mars mines (He-3 extraction)

### Surface geography

The Mars He-3 mines are clustered in three regions:

- **Northern pole mining cluster.** Open-pit helium-catchment. Most fatalities. NA conscripts work here.
- **Equatorial processing cluster.** He-3 catchment-to-fuel plant. Russia conscripts work here.
- **Southern mobile-mining cluster.** Smaller, lower-grade ore. Less fatalities. Mixed conscripts.

### The hidden mining claims

Some He-3 deposits exist *outside* the Trust's licensed claims. These are *concealed* — the Trust doesn't know they exist, OR the Trust knows but hasn't gotten around to extraction.

Hiding these deposits is part of the dismantling arc. When discovered, *they open the architecture cracks.*

### The trapping system

The He-3 mines uniquely needed skilled labor that is *isolated from the surface.* The mines are designed so that labor cannot leave without passing through the Trust's records. Escape from Mars is *almost impossible.*

Most conscripts do not escape. The desertion rate is low, *because the system is set up that way.*

## He-3 dismantling arc

### What dismantling means, mechanically

The system has discrete *discovery acts*. Each act unlocks when a captain triggers it. The act is permanent once unlocked. Discovery acts are:

| Act | Description | Unlocks |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Cartel structure mapped | Industrial-tier dispatches |
| 2 | Trust sabotage programs found | Mid-tier industrial arcs |
| 3 | Bunker-state deposits mapped | Alt-fuel possibilities |
| 4 | Alternative cartography completed | Mid-run miracle encounters |
| 5 | The Trustee's *final* project queued | Endgame |

### Discovery acts progression (rough)

**Act 1: Cartel structure mapped.**
This is the player learning *who owns what.* It can be done by meeting a T5 flunky early, by reading financial paperwork overheard in narrative, or by passing through specific station caches.

**Act 2: Trust sabotage programs found.**
Records of the sabotage program. *Who sabotaged which government,* and what equipment was held. This Act 2 unlocks once Act 1 is in play.

**Act 3: Bunker-state deposits mapped.**
Hidden deposits *outside* the Trust's claims. These deposits are *the durable resource.* Once a captain *finds one,* the deposit is permanent.

**Act 4: Alternative cartography completed.**
The Captain now knows *enough* to plan large-scale endgames. Fuel-replacement options begin. Some become viable.

**Act 5: The Trustee's *final* project queued.**
The endgame. Whatever the Trustee's *final* project is, it depends on Acts 1–4 completed.

### The Player's *Call* across runs

Each captain's actions contribute to one or more acts. *Which* captain does *which* act is up to the player. The Trustee has preferences, but the AI does not enforce them.

The Trustee's preference map (placeholder, TBD):

- Run 1: Police nothing. Let the captain discover Act 1.
- Run 4: Encourage Act 2 paths.
- Run 9: Push Act 3 timing forward.
- Run 16: Push Act 4.
- Run 22: Begin Act 5 prelim.

These are *gentle pushes* not requirements. The AI nudges the encounter pool toward acts the campaign needs.

## The Trust's structural vulnerabilities

The dismantling matters because the Trust's structure is *brittle in specific places:*

- **T1 (Resource)** is fractious.
- **T2 (Shipping)** has weak inheritance authority.
- **T4 (Biotech)** signed the genetic-program documents. *Can this be exposed in a future generation?*
- **T5 (Finance)** profits from insurance payouts. *Whistleblowers in T5 could be devastating.*
- **T3 (Habitat)** has the conscript-mortality records. *Most guilty. Most aware.*
- **T6 (Heavy)** has visible conscript-workforce. *Public-opinion-attackable.*
- **T7 (Energy)** is the core cartel. *Their survival is most-brittle when monopoly breaks.*

The dismantling arc exploits these. Discovery acts tilt attention toward one or more vulnerabilities.
